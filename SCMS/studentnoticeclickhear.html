Experiment 1 – Crystallography

Aim

To study the crystal structure and determine lattice parameters using X-ray diffraction.

Apparatus Required

  • X-ray diffractometer
  • Crystal specimen
  • Measuring instruments
  • Graph sheet

Theory

Crystallography is the study of crystal structures and their properties. Crystals are solids whose atoms are arranged in a highly ordered, repeating pattern. The smallest repeating unit in a crystal structure is called a unit cell.

When X-rays fall on a crystal, they are diffracted by the atomic planes. The condition for constructive interference is given by Bragg’s Law.

Bragg’s Law

nλ = 2d sinθ

Where:

  • n = Order of reflection
  • λ = Wavelength of X-rays
  • d = Interplanar spacing
  • θ = Glancing angle

Miller Indices

Miller indices are a set of three integers (h k l) used to denote crystal planes. They are determined by taking reciprocals of the intercepts made by the plane on crystallographic axes.

Procedure

  • Mount the crystal on the X-ray diffractometer.
  • Adjust the angle of incidence.
  • Record diffraction angles.
  • Calculate interplanar spacing using Bragg’s Law.
  • Determine lattice parameter.

Calculations

For cubic crystals:

d = a / √(h² + k² + l²)

Where:

  • a = Lattice constant
  • h, k, l = Miller indices

Result

The lattice parameter of the given crystal is determined successfully.

Conclusion

The experiment verifies Bragg’s Law and helps in understanding crystal structure analysis using X-ray diffraction.
